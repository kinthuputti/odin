CHICKEN BIRYANI

DESCRIPTION

Chicken biryani is a flavorful and aromatic one-pot dish that combines fragrant rice, tender chicken, and a medley of spices. Originating from the Indian subcontinent, it is a dish loved for its rich flavors, vibrant colors, and layers of texture.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 ½ tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 (3 inch) cinnamon stick
  • 1 pod cardamom
  • 1 clove
  • 4 medium onions, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons chopped fresh garlic
  • ¼ teaspoon chopped fresh ginger
  • 1 pound boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into cubes
  • 1 teaspoon red chile powder, or to taste
  • 1 ½ medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 16 ounces plain yogurt
  • 3 tablespoons water
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice, or to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup white rice
  • 1 teaspoon butter
  • salt to taste
  • 1 bunch fresh mint, chopped
  • 1 bunch chopped cilantro

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  2. Heat oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Add cinnamon stick, cardamom, and clove; cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add onions and sauté until translucent and beginning to turn golden, 5 to 8 minutes. Add garlic and ginger; cook and stir until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add chicken and sauté for 3 minutes. Stir in chili powder and cook for 2 to 3 minutes. Add tomatoes; cook and stir until juices release, about 5 minutes. Remove from the heat.
  4. Mix yogurt, 3 tablespoons water, and lemon juice together in a bowl; stir into the chicken mixture until incorporated.
  5. Cover and bake in the preheated oven until sauce is somewhat thickened and concentrated, about 15 minutes.
  6. Meanwhile, bring 2 cups water and rice to a boil in a saucepan. Reduce the heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until rice is about halfway cooked, about 7 minutes. Drain.
  7. Remove the chicken mixture from the oven. Stir in half-cooked rice, butter, and salt, then cover and bake for 1 hour. Stir in mint and cilantro before serving.
